7-Step Carbon Methodology — CFI CCER-Ready v1.2GRI co-developed
STEP 1
Boundary Definition & Functional Unit
Scope 3 Category 11 (use of sold products — tyres in operation) and Category 12 (end-of-life tyre treatment via retreading).
Functional unit: gCO₂ per vehicle-kilometre (gCO₂/vkm).
Geographic boundary: mainland China (CCER jurisdiction).
Baseline period: first 90 days of sensor deployment per vehicle.
Baseline stratified by: vehicle class, route type, load factor, season.
STEP 2
Emissions Reduction Quantification
ER = Σ [ (BIi − AIi) × Di × Ni ]

Where:
  ER   = Total emissions reductions (tCO₂e)
  BIi = Baseline intensity, segment i (gCO₂/vkm)
  AIi = Actual monitored intensity, segment i (gCO₂/vkm)
  Di  = Distance travelled, segment i (km)
  Ni  = Number of vehicles, segment i
Retreading module: CO₂_retread = N_retread × EF_avoided
EF_avoided = 30.0 kgCO₂/tyre (conservative: 40% discount on verified LCA diff of ~56.7 kgCO₂).
New tyre manufacture: 72.0 kgCO₂ · Retread process: 15.3 kgCO₂ · Source: USTMA LCA 2021.
STEP 3
Digital Monitoring, Reporting & Verification (MRV)
Data sources: Smart Predictive Tyre sensors (pressure, wear, temp every 15 min) → G7 telematics GPS (route, speed, load) → Engine OBD fuel consumption (Priority 1, where available).
Carbon Ledger: Every reading SHA-256 hashed before writing to Alibaba BaaS blockchain. Any alteration is cryptographically detectable. 24,841+ entries as of Q2 2026.
API access: Bureau Veritas and SGS granted read-only API access to the full Carbon Ledger for independent verification sampling.
GRI auto-generation: GRI 305/306 ESG reports auto-generated from Carbon Ledger every quarter. CSRC disclosure package auto-generated from Month 1.
STEP 4
Leakage Assessment
Three leakage sources assessed:
• Increased tyre production elsewhere: Retreading reduces new tyre demand. Any displacement reduces net emissions. Negative leakage — conservatively ignored.
• Rebound effect: Fleet operators run fixed contract schedules. No incremental driving observed in comparable G7 deployments. Negligible. No deduction applied.
• Upstream retreading inputs: Captured in the 40% discount on EF_avoided. No additional deduction.
Overall leakage: negligible. No deduction applied to ER_calculated.
STEP 5
Uncertainty & Conservativeness Adjustment
Source-specific uncertainty assessments:
• Tyre sensor pressure accuracy: ±0.5 PSI (calibration protocol, outlier removal)
• Fuel estimation model (Priority 2, no OBD): ±8–12% vs direct (Priority 1 OBD used where available)
• Retreading EF: ±15% (LCA range) — 40% conservative discount applied
• Route segment classification: ±5% (GPS validated against G7 road type DB)
ER_final = ER_calculated × (1 − 0.05)
5% blanket conservative adjustment applied on top of all source-specific discounts.
ER_final = credits submitted to Beijing Green Exchange for CCER issuance.
STEP 6
Dual Verification Protocol — Structural Independence
Bureau Veritas (Carbon Credit Verification): CCER methodology adherence, ER calculation, additionality, credit issuance. 1 of 5 MEE-accredited CCER v2 verifiers. Engaged by fleet operator, not Michelin.

SGS — CONFIRMED (Data & MRV Verification): Sensor data integrity, Carbon Ledger audit, monitoring protocol compliance. 93,000 employees · 2,600 labs · CNAS + CMA accredited · Largest HDL auditor in China.

Why structural independence matters: Michelin cannot alter Carbon Ledger data (SHA-256 cryptographic immutability). Neither verifier is engaged by Michelin. Conflict of interest is architecturally impossible, not just contractually avoided.
STEP 7
Credit Issuance & Ownership Structure
• Credit unit: 1 CCER = 1 tonne CO₂e reduced (verified).
• Issuance frequency: Annual (calendar year). Triggered by dual verification sign-off.
• Ownership: Fleet operator earns 100% of CCER credits.
• Michelin fee: 8–12% methodology fee at point of trade only. Zero upfront.
• Trading platform: Beijing Green Exchange (BGE) — API-integrated with Carbon Ledger.
• Open standard commitment: Methodology published at Phase 2 start. Jointly owned by GRI, Michelin, and Phase 2 co-developer partners. Any logistics operator globally can adopt it.
